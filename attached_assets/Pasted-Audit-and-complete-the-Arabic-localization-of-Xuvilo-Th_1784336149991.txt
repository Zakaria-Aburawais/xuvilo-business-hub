Audit and complete the Arabic localization of Xuvilo.

The Arabic interface currently contains untranslated English labels and descriptions, including examples such as:

- Most Popular
- Free to Use
- Document Templates
- Currency Converter
- Stamp Maker
- Business Card Maker
- Company Profile Maker
- Temp Email
- Document Tracker
- Import Cost
- Break-Even
- Markup vs Margin
- Loan Calculator
- Invoice Aging
- The English announcement bar
- English descriptions inside Arabic cards and sections

Requirements:

1. Search the entire application for user-facing hard-coded English text rendered while the active language is Arabic.
2. Move hard-coded interface text into the existing translation system.
3. Provide accurate, natural Arabic translations—not literal or machine-like wording.
4. Preserve English brand names only where genuinely necessary.
5. Keep numbers, currencies, punctuation, icons, and technical terms properly aligned in RTL.
6. Ensure Arabic and English pages communicate identical features, prices, limits, and disclaimers.
7. Check navigation menus, mobile menus, dropdowns, tool cards, calculator cards, pricing cards, footer links, cookie banner, dialogs, validation messages, error messages, loading messages, tooltips, buttons, metadata, and structured data.
8. Do not translate customer-entered content or alter URLs unless the routing system is specifically designed for localized URLs.
9. Add a fallback mechanism so missing Arabic translation keys do not silently display unfinished keys or inappropriate English content.

Test at desktop and mobile widths: 320, 375, 390, 768, 1024, and 1366 pixels.

Do not report completion until you provide:

- Every modified translation and component file.
- A list of all newly translated strings.
- Screenshots of the Arabic homepage at desktop and mobile widths.
- Evidence that no unintended English interface text remains.
- Production-build and test results.
- Confirmation that the English interface was not damaged.
